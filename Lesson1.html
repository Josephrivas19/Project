Importance of IT Security

IT Security, also known as Information Security or Cybersecurity, is the practice of safeguarding computer systems, networks, and data from unauthorized access, theft, or damage. It ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of information and builds trust.

Information Assurance

Information Assurance manages risks related to the use, processing, storage, and transmission of data. It protects integrity, availability, authenticity, non-repudiation, and confidentiality.

Violation of Security Principles

The CIA Triad guides policies to protect information:

  • Confidentiality: Restrict access to authorized individuals only.
  • Integrity: Keep information accurate and unaltered.
  • Availability: Ensure data is accessible when needed.

Career Opportunities in IT Security

IT security professionals protect networks and systems from cyber threats. Roles include monitoring, incident response, and more.

Red Team vs Blue Team

Red Team (Offensive Security)

  • Hack things before threat actors do.
  • Simulate real-world attacks and exploit vulnerabilities.
  • Identify system weaknesses before attackers do.

Blue Team (Defensive Security)

  • Improve security gaps before adversaries find them.
  • Monitor traffic for suspicious activity.
  • Implement firewalls and defensive measures.

Career Opportunities in Cybersecurity

  • Security Analyst: Monitors and responds to security issues.
  • Penetration Tester: Tests networks for vulnerabilities.
  • Security Engineer: Designs secure systems.
  • Incident Responder: Handles cyberattacks.
  • Cybersecurity Consultant: Advises on best practices.
  • Forensic Expert: Investigates cybercrimes.
  • CISO: Leads the organization's security strategy.

Security Operations Center (SOC)

  • Monitors and analyzes security events.
  • Identifies and responds to threats.
  • Protects the organization from cyberattacks.

Three Pillars of Cybersecurity

People

Awareness and training to strengthen security culture within the organization.

Processes

Defined security procedures and incident response plans to handle threats effectively.

Technology

Tools and solutions to protect systems, networks, and sensitive data.

Vulnerability Scanning

  • Systematic review of security weaknesses.
  • Performed with automated tools.
  • Reports should be manually validated.

Penetration Testing

  • Simulated cyberattack to check for vulnerabilities.
  • Verify genuine vulnerabilities by actively exploiting them.
  • Test IT staff response and policy implementation.

Penetration Testing Life Cycle

  1. Reconnaissance: Passive info gathering like Whois, Google Dorks.
  2. Enumeration / Scanning: Active scanning and vulnerability probing.
  3. Gaining Access: Exploit vulnerabilities to enter.
  4. Maintaining Access: Persistence via backdoors or C2.
  5. Covering Tracks: Remove logs and traces.
  6. Reporting: Document findings and fixes.
